# Energy Modelling Tools

Welcome to the **Energy Modelling Tools** organization! This is a central hub for developing and managing introduction websites and documentation for various energy and climate modeling tools focused on climate-compatible growth, energy planning, and sustainable development.

This organization hosts GitHub Pages sites that provide comprehensive documentation, tutorials, and resources for open-source tools supporting energy access, climate finance, infrastructure planning, and sustainable transitions.

## üöÄ Hosted Pages

### [OnSSET - Open-Source Spatial Electrification Tool](https://energy-modeling-tools.github.io/onsset/)

OnSSET is a GIS-based optimization tool developed to support electrification planning and decision-making for achieving energy access goals in currently unserved locations. 

**Key Features:**
- Bottom-up optimization energy modeling
- Spatially explicit electrification analysis
- Support for conventional and renewable energy technologies
- Open-source and modular structure
- Cost-effective electrification planning

**Use Cases:**
- National and regional electrification planning
- Policy formulation and research
- Least-cost technology deployment analysis
- SDG7 (Sustainable Development Goal 7) achievement support

### [OnStove - Open-Source Clean Cooking Model](https://energy-modeling-tools.github.io/onstove/)

OnStove is an open-source spatial cost-benefit model for evaluating and prioritising clean cooking solutions. It helps identify where and how modern energy access interventions can deliver the greatest social, economic, and environmental benefits.

**Key Features:**
- Spatial cost-benefit analysis for clean cooking
- Technology comparison and optimization
- Co-benefits quantification (health, emissions, time savings)
- Support for national clean cooking strategies

**Use Cases:**
- Identifying least-cost clean cooking transitions
- Supporting national clean cooking strategies
- Quantifying social and environmental co-benefits
- Investment prioritization for clean cooking access

### [FINPLAN - Model for Financial Analysis of Electric Sector Expansion Plans](https://energy-modeling-tools.github.io/finplan/)

FINPLAN is a model for financial analysis of electric sector expansion plans, assessing the financial viability of power projects by analyzing costs, revenues, and funding sources over time.

**Key Features:**
- Financial viability assessment for power projects
- Financing strategy comparison (loans, equity, grants)
- Cash flow and debt service evaluation
- Risk identification and sensitivity analysis

**Use Cases:**
- Assessing financial viability of power projects
- Comparing financing strategies and their impacts
- Evaluating cash flow, debt service, and profitability
- Supporting investment decisions for bankable projects
- Complementing techno-economic models with financial realism

### [FinTrack - Climate Finance Tracking Tool](https://energy-modeling-tools.github.io/fintrack/)

FinTrack is a tool directed at addressing the lack of clarity around the allocation and availability of climate funds to countries, using historic data and indicative allocations to highlight resources available to address climate financing gaps.

**Key Features:**
- Historic climate fund allocation tracking
- Indicative allocation analysis
- Fund accessibility assessment
- Barrier identification (concessionality, criteria)

**Use Cases:**
- Identifying accessible climate finance
- Prioritizing climate fund opportunities
- Assessing financial barriers to fund access
- Increasing financial feasibility of sustainable transitions

### [NISMOD - National Infrastructure Systems Model](https://energy-modeling-tools.github.io/nismod/)

NISMOD is a collection of tools and libraries for the analysis and modelling of infrastructure systems, originally developed as part of the ITRC/MISTRAL project.

**Key Features:**
- Integrated infrastructure system modeling
- System-of-systems framework
- Climate risk and resilience analysis
- Multi-sectoral infrastructure planning

**Use Cases:**
- Integrated infrastructure planning
- Climate risk assessment
- Infrastructure resilience analysis
- Cross-sectoral dependency analysis

### [FFRM - Fossil Fuel Retirement Model](https://energy-modeling-tools.github.io/ffrm/)

FFRM is an open-source Python-based fossil fuel retirement model, originally developed by the World Bank as a coal retirement optimization model.

**Key Features:**
- Fossil fuel power plant retirement optimization
- Stranded asset cost estimation
- Transition pathway analysis
- Financial impact assessment

**Use Cases:**
- Estimating stranded costs of retiring fossil fuel plants
- Planning just energy transitions
- Optimizing retirement timing and sequencing
- Supporting decarbonization policy decisions

### [MINFin - Model for Informed National Financing](https://energy-modeling-tools.github.io/minfin/)

MINFin is a model for informed national financing that assesses the financial viability of energy transitions and aligns investment plans with available concessional finance.

**Key Features:**
- Financial viability assessment for energy transitions
- Climate finance needs estimation
- Concessional finance alignment
- Country-specific cost of capital analysis

**Use Cases:**
- Financing strategy development for energy plans
- Estimating climate finance needs for LMICs
- Disaggregating global climate goals into national needs
- Supporting "access to affordability" policy frameworks

### [PathCalc - Pathways Calculator](https://energy-modeling-tools.github.io/pathcalc/)

PathCalc is an interactive tool for exploring thousands of potential pathways, leveraging ambition levels and levers to show how various strategies affect emissions, costs, and energy.

**Key Features:**
- Interactive pathway exploration
- Ambition level scenario modeling
- Emissions and cost impact analysis
- Sector-specific scenario analysis

**Use Cases:**
- Exploring emissions reduction pathways
- Comparing strategy impacts on emissions and costs
- Supporting transport sector planning
- Facilitating participatory scenario development
